Reserve Bank of India (RBI)
Circular on Know Your Customer (KYC) Guidelines

1. Purpose
This circular consolidates and updates the Know Your Customer (KYC) norms applicable to all regulated entities including banks, NBFCs, and financial institutions.

2. Mandatory KYC Requirements
- Permanent Account Number (PAN) is mandatory for opening any savings or current account.
- Aadhaar may be used as an officially valid document (OVD) for identity and address verification.
- If PAN is not available, Form 60 must be submitted temporarily, subject to later verification.

3. Address Verification
- Valid address proof must be obtained before account activation.
- Acceptable address proof includes Aadhaar, Passport, Voter ID, or Driving License.

4. Customer Risk Categorization
Customers must be categorized as:
- Low Risk
- Medium Risk
- High Risk

High-risk customers include:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Customers with incomplete KYC
- Accounts with unusually high transaction volumes

5. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
For high-risk customers:
- Additional verification is mandatory
- Senior management approval is required
- Transaction monitoring must be enhanced

6. Non-Compliance
Accounts with incomplete KYC must be restricted within 30 days.
Failure to comply may lead to account suspension.
