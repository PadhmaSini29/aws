# Wildfire Trends Analysis (1993–2022)

## Overview
This document summarizes long-term wildfire trends in the United States from 1993 to 2022.
It focuses on wildfire frequency, total area burned, and notable changes over time.

---

## Key Statistics

- **Average annual wildfires (1990s):** ~78,600 fires
- **Average annual wildfires (since 2000):** ~70,000 fires
- **Average acres burned per year (1990s):** ~3.3 million acres
- **Average acres burned per year (since 2000):** ~7.0 million acres

Although the number of fires has slightly decreased, the **area burned has more than doubled**.

---

## Trend Summary

- Wildfire frequency remained relatively stable from 1993 to 2022.
- A sharp increase in total acres burned is observed after the year 2000.
- Recent years (2020–2022) show some of the highest wildfire impacts on record.

---

## Contributing Factors

Several factors contribute to increased wildfire severity:

1. Rising average temperatures
2. Prolonged drought conditions
3. Earlier snowmelt and longer fire seasons
4. Changes in land use and forest management practices

---

## Conclusion

While wildfire counts have not increased significantly, the **severity and impact of wildfires have grown substantially**.
This trend highlights the importance of monitoring not just the number of fires, but also their scale and intensity.
